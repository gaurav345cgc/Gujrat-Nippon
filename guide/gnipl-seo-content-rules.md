## RULE 4 — KEYWORD PLACEMENT WITHIN BODY COPY

When writing or editing any body copy section, follow this placement checklist for the page's primary keyword:

- [ ] Used **once in the first sentence or first paragraph** of the page
- [ ] Used **2 to 4 times** total throughout the page body (natural repetition, not forced)
- [ ] Used **once in at least one H2** heading on the page
- [ ] Used **once in the image alt text** of the main image on that page
- [ ] Used **in the URL slug** for that page (see Rule 6)

**What not to do:**
- Do not repeat the exact keyword phrase more than 4 times — Google penalises keyword stuffing
- Do not use the same primary keyword on two different pages — this causes cannibalization where pages compete against each other in search

## RULE 5 — H2 AND H3 STRUCTURE RULES

H2 and H3 headings serve two purposes: they structure the page for the reader, and they signal topic relevance to Google. Follow these rules:

- **H2s** should describe major sections of the page and contain secondary keywords naturally
- **H3s** should describe subsections or list items
- Every product or industry subpage must have a minimum of **3 H2 sections**
- Headings must be direct and functional, matching the content guide's style — no cleverness, no taglines

**Required H2 structure for product subpages (Turnkey, Spares, Chemicals, Equipment, Moulding):**

```
H1: [Primary keyword — page title]

H2: What We Supply / Our [Product] Range
  (list the specific products/equipment with 2–3 lines each)

H2: Industries We Cater To
  (list the relevant sectors for that product)

H2: Why Choose Gujarat Nippon for [Product Category]
  (3–5 bullet points: experience, quality, sourcing network, execution)

CTA paragraph: contact line in formal B2B tone
```

**Required H2 structure for industry subpages:**

```
H1: [Industry Name] Solutions — Gujarat Nippon International

H2: What We Supply to the [Industry] Sector
  (specific products and services relevant to that industry)

H2: Our Capabilities in This Sector
  (track record, geographies, execution)

H2: Related Products & Services
  (internal links to product pages)

CTA paragraph
```

| `/products/equipment` | `/products/capital-equipment` |
| `/products/logic-plastics` | `/products/logic-plastics-manufacturing` |

Rules for slugs:
- All lowercase
- Words separated by hyphens, never underscores or spaces
- No special characters
- Keep it short — 3 to 5 words maximum
- **Only change slugs before launch.** After launch, changing a URL requires a 301 redirect or it breaks inbound links and loses ranking.

## RULE 7 — IMAGE ALT TEXT

Every image on the site must have an `alt` attribute that describes the image using relevant keywords. This serves both Google image indexing and screen reader accessibility.

**Format:**
```
alt="[What the image shows] — [context or company name]"
```

**Rules:**
- Never leave alt text blank
- Never use the filename as the alt text (e.g. `alt="product_1.png"` is wrong)
- Never keyword-stuff alt text — one natural phrase is enough
- Alt text must describe what is actually in the image, not what you wish it showed

## RULE 10 — WHAT TO INCLUDE IN PRODUCT SUBPAGE BODY COPY

Each product subpage must cover these points to satisfy both the reader and Google's quality assessment:

1. **What it is** — a plain definition of the product/service in industrial terms
2. **What GNIPL specifically supplies** — named products, equipment types, specifications where available (e.g. "cold rolling mills, hot rolling mills, tube mills, strip galvanizing lines, deep drawing presses")
3. **Who it is for** — industries and buyer types (e.g. "steel processing plants, metal rolling facilities, manufacturing companies in India, Africa, and the GCC")
4. **How GNIPL delivers it** — turnkey basis, sourcing network, installation support, after-sales
5. **Why GNIPL** — brief, formal, factual (experience, project count, network, quality)
6. **A contact CTA** — formal B2B style per the content guide

**Do not pad these pages with generic industry background information.** Every paragraph must relate directly to what GNIPL does or supplies.

## RULE 11 — SCHEMA MARKUP (ADD TO CODE, NOT CONTENT)

These are code-level additions that developers add to the page — not visible to readers. Include them in the codebase before launch:

- **`Organization` schema** on the homepage: includes company name, address, phone, email, founding year, CIN
- **`BreadcrumbList` schema** on all inner pages: helps Google show page path in search results
- **`Product` schema** on each product subpage: includes product name, description, brand (Gujarat Nippon)
- **`FAQPage` schema** on the FAQ page (once created): each question and answer marked up

These do not affect the written content. Pass these instructions to the developer.

## RULE 13 — WHAT NOT TO DO (SEO-SPECIFIC DON'TS)

These are common mistakes that harm ranking. Cursor must avoid all of them:

| Don't | Why |
|-------|-----|
| Use the same title tag on two pages | Causes duplicate content signal; Google picks one to rank and ignores the other |
| Use the same meta description on multiple pages | Currently every page has the same one — this must be fixed |
| Leave alt text blank on any image | Wasted indexing opportunity; also an accessibility violation |
| Use keyword-stuffed headings like `"Cold Rolling Mill Cold Rolling Mill Supplier Cold Rolling"` | Google penalises unnatural repetition |
| Use the same primary keyword on two different product pages | Pages compete against each other in search results |
| Link to a page using only `"click here"` or `"learn more"` as anchor text | Google cannot determine the destination page topic from these anchors |
| Write product pages with fewer than 300 words | Thin content is actively demoted in rankings |
| Leave any `"Learn More"` button pointing to a dead end or back to the same page | Creates crawl dead-ends and high bounce rate |
| Use the current homepage H1 (`"Engineering Precision Systems For Global Industries"`) | Contains no keyword; tells Google nothing about the business |
| Keep the SaaS placeholder specs (`"Cloud, On-Premise, Hybrid"`) on product pages | Irrelevant content harms the entire domain's quality score |
